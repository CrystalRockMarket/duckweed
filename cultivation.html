System Architecture: The Laminar Flow Rack

For personal production, volume is irrelevant; surface area is king

Design Principle: Wolffia only inhabits the top 2-3mm of the water column. A vertical rack system maximizes surface area while minimizing footprint.

Tray Geometry

Shallow trays (10-15cm depth) provide thermal mass stability without wasting water and nutrients. HDPE or food-grade Polypropylene (PP) trays are required to prevent plasticizer leaching.

Recommended: 40" x 20" x 4" deep food-grade trays with opaque bottoms to prevent algal growth.

Fluid Dynamics

A "slow-flow" laminar system prevents nutrient stratification without disturbing the delicate fronds. Total volume turnover of 2-4 times per day is optimal.

The inlet should be diffused (spray bar below surface) to prevent surface agitation that can submerge plants.

Air Movement

Stagnant air encourages fungal growth (Pythium) and limits CO‚ÇÇ supply. Gentle oscillating fans move air across the canopy without disturbing the water surface.

Lighting Strategy

Full-spectrum white LED (4000K-6500K) at 150-300 ¬µmol/m¬≤/s PFD. Supplemental blue light (450nm) enhances anthocyanin and phenolic production.

Photoperiod: 16/8 or 18/6 cycle balances growth with energy efficiency.

Biological Risk Management

Safety protocols for contamination prevention

Critical: BMAA (Œ≤-N-methylamino-L-alanine) is a neurotoxin produced by cyanobacteria that can bioaccumulate in duckweed. Strict contamination control is essential.

Cyanobacteria Prevention

  • Use opaque materials for all water containers
  • Maintain dense duckweed canopy to shade water
  • Start with verified axenic (sterile) culture
  • Perform 0.1% hydrogen peroxide dip (5-10 min) on new cultures
  • Never introduce pond or wild water to the system

Pest Management (IPM)

Aphids and fungus gnats are primary threats. No chemical pesticides can be used on a whole-plant food crop.

  • Physical exclusion: Grow tents, intake filters on fans
  • Biological controls: Predatory nematodes for gnat larvae
  • Yellow sticky traps for monitoring
  • Neem oil (food-grade) as last resort for aphids

Heavy Metal Control

Duckweed hyperaccumulates heavy metals. Control points:

  • Use only RO or distilled water
  • Food-grade minerals only (no agricultural-grade)
  • Test water source annually if using well water
  • Avoid metal components in contact with nutrient solution

Sterile Technique

  • Wash hands thoroughly before handling
  • Sanitize tools with 70% isopropyl alcohol
  • Keep pets away from grow area
  • Quarantine new cultures for 1 week before introduction

Troubleshooting Guide

Visual diagnosis of common cultivation problems

Problem: Yellowing (Chlorosis)

Symptoms: Fronds turn pale green to yellow, starting with older tissue

Likely Cause: Iron deficiency or nitrogen limitation

Solution

Check pH first‚Äîiron becomes unavailable above pH 7.0. If pH is correct, supplement with chelated iron (Fe-DTPA) at 1.0 ¬µM concentration.

Shop Chelated Iron

Problem: Algae Growth

Symptoms: Green film on tray walls, cloudy water, competing with Wolffia

Likely Cause: Light penetration into water column

Solution

Use opaque trays, maintain dense Wolffia cover, reduce light intensity or photoperiod. Consider UV sterilizer in reservoir line.

Shop UV Sterilizers

Problem: Slow Growth

Symptoms: Doubling time >5 days, sparse coverage

Likely Cause: Temperature, light, or nutrient limitation

Solution

Verify temperature is 22-28¬∞C. Check light intensity with PAR meter (target 150-300 ¬µmol). Test EC and adjust nutrients.

Shop PAR Meters

Problem: Browning/Rotting

Symptoms: Brown patches, foul odor, sinking fronds

Likely Cause: Anaerobic conditions or bacterial infection

Solution

Increase aeration and water circulation. Harvest more frequently to prevent overcrowding. Check for Pythium (cottony growth).

Problem: Turion Formation

Symptoms: Sinking, starch-dense structures instead of floating fronds

Likely Cause: Phosphate starvation, low temperature, or high stress

Solution

Ensure phosphate levels 30-50 ppm. Maintain temperature >17¬∞C. Check for consistent nutrient availability.
